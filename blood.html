Blood

Blood is essential part of life. Blood Circulates through our body and delivers essentilas substances like oxygen and nutrients to the body's cells. It also transport metabolic waste products away from those same cells. There is no substitute to blood. It can neither be made nor be manufactured. Only source of blood in need is generous blood donors.Blood carries carbon dioxide and other waste materials to the lungs, kidneys, and digestive system to be removed from the body. Blood also fights infections, and carries hormones around the bodys.The components of blood include red blood cells and white blood cells, and cell fragments called platelets along with plasma which is liquid portion, that contains water, proteins, salts, lipids, and glucose.

Blood Groups and components

Blood groups are genetic, like the colour of a person’s eyes. There are four major blood groups: A, B, AB and O. Your type in the ABO system and Rhesus (Rh) factor are important in blood types. Human blood type is determined by antigens on the red blood cells. An antigen is a structure on the cell surface that causes a human immune response reacts to if the structure is foreign to the person’s body. As a result, blood type match is of critical importance. The donor’s blood type is identified at the Blood Bank , and the patient’s blood type is determined before transfusion.

Blood Groups and Rhesus (Rh)

Group A has one type of antigen (A), Group B has a different type (B), Group AB has both A and B antigens, and Group O does not have either antigen. These blood groups are further divided by whether or not an antigen called the Rh factor is present on the person’s blood cells.A person’s Rh group can be positive (+) or negative (-).This depends on the existence of antigen RhD on the red blood cells. If the RhD antigen is present, a person is Rh positive and if RhD is not present, the person is Rh negative.

Combining Rh factor with the four major blood types results in eight different main blood types (for example, AB-). In addition, there are minor blood groups.An individual of a certain blood type will develop antibodies against the antigens that they don’t have. For example, a Blood Group A individual has no B antigens on their red blood cells; therefore, this person’s white blood cells will make antibodies against the B antigen (anti-B) that will be present in their plasma.

In transfusion medicine, it is very important that patients receive red blood cells that are compatible with their blood type. It is also important that plasma recipients are not transfused with plasma that contains antibodies that will destroy their red blood cells.

If a person is Rh negative, they may develop antibodies on exposure to Rh positive blood (such as during transfusions or pregnancy). These antibodies may cause pregnancy complications for Rh negative women giving birth to an Rh positive child. In addition, about 30 other blood group systems have been identified besides the ABO and Rh blood group systems. Of these, the clinically most significant are Kell, Kidd and Duffy systems. Donors are also tested for Kell blood group.

Why Blood ?

  • Being treated for cancer
  • Undergoing orthopedic surgeries
  • Undergoing cardiovascular surgeries
  • Being treated for inherited blood disorders
